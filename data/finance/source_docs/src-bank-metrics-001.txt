Bank Financial Performance Metrics

RETURN ON EQUITY (ROE):
ROE = Net Income / Average Shareholders' Equity
Measures profitability relative to equity capital invested.
Canadian Big Six banks target ROE of 15-18% in normal conditions.
ROE below 10% is considered weak; above 18% is exceptional.

RETURN ON ASSETS (ROA):
ROA = Net Income / Average Total Assets
Measures efficiency of asset base in generating profits.
Banks typically have ROA of 0.8-1.2% due to highly leveraged balance sheets.
Lower than most industries because bank assets are primarily loans and securities with thin margins.

RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ROE AND ROA:
ROE = ROA × Equity Multiplier (Total Assets / Shareholders' Equity)
Example: ROA of 1.0% × Equity Multiplier of 15x = ROE of 15%
Banks' high leverage (assets typically 10-20x equity) amplifies ROA into a much higher ROE.
This is why banks appear highly profitable on ROE despite modest ROA.

NET INTEREST MARGIN (NIM):
NIM = Net Interest Income / Average Interest-Earning Assets
Measures the spread between interest earned on loans/investments and interest paid on deposits/borrowings.
Canadian bank NIMs typically 1.5-2.5%.

EFFICIENCY RATIO:
Efficiency Ratio = Non-Interest Expenses / Revenue (NII + Non-Interest Income)
Lower is better. Canadian Big Six target below 55%.
Measures how much of revenue is consumed by operating costs.

PRICE-TO-BOOK (P/B) RATIO:
Common valuation metric for banks: Market Price per Share / Book Value per Share.
Canadian banks typically trade at 1.3-2.0x book. Below 1.0x suggests market concerns about asset quality or earnings sustainability.
