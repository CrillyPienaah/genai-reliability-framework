Basel III Liquidity Standards — LCR and NSFR

LIQUIDITY COVERAGE RATIO (LCR):
The LCR requires banks to hold sufficient High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA) to survive a 30-day stressed outflow scenario.

Formula: LCR = Stock of HQLA / Total Net Cash Outflows over 30 days ≥ 100%

HQLA Definition:
Level 1 assets (0% haircut): cash, central bank reserves, sovereign bonds (0% risk weight)
Level 2A assets (15% haircut): certain sovereign bonds, covered bonds, high-grade corporate bonds
Level 2B assets (25-50% haircut): lower-rated corporate bonds, equity securities (subject to caps)
Level 2 assets capped at 40% of total HQLA.

Net Cash Outflows = Cash Outflows - min(Cash Inflows, 75% of Cash Outflows)
Outflow rates vary by liability type: retail deposits 3-10%, wholesale funding 25-100%, committed credit facilities 10-30%.

OSFI requires Canadian D-SIBs to maintain LCR ≥ 100% at all times.

NET STABLE FUNDING RATIO (NSFR):
The NSFR addresses structural liquidity risk over a one-year horizon.

Formula: NSFR = Available Stable Funding (ASF) / Required Stable Funding (RSF) ≥ 100%

ASF components (weighted by stability factor): equity and long-term debt >1yr (100%), stable retail deposits (95%), less stable deposits (90%), wholesale funding <6 months (50%), short-term wholesale funding <3 months (0-50%).

RSF components reflect funding requirements: HQLA (0-5% RSF), residential mortgages (65%), other loans (85-100%), off-balance-sheet commitments (5-10%).

KEY DIFFERENCE FROM LCR:
LCR = 30-day stress horizon (short-term liquidity survival)
NSFR = 1-year structural funding horizon (sustainable funding profile)
Both ratios are required and complementary.
