OSFI Guideline B-20 — Residential Mortgage Underwriting Practices

PURPOSE:
B-20 sets out OSFI's expectations for sound residential mortgage underwriting and related risk management practices at federally regulated financial institutions (FRFIs).

MORTGAGE STRESS TEST (KEY REQUIREMENT):
All new uninsured residential mortgages must be underwritten at the higher of:
(a) The Bank of Canada's 5-year conventional mortgage rate (benchmark rate), or
(b) The contract rate plus 200 basis points (2.0%)

This stress test ensures borrowers can continue servicing their mortgage if interest rates rise above the contracted rate.

STRESS TEST APPLICABILITY:
The stress test APPLIES to:
- New uninsured mortgage originations
- Refinances (changes to loan amount, amortization, or adding equity take-out)
- Switches to a different federally regulated lender at renewal
- New draws on existing home equity lines of credit (HELOCs)

The stress test does NOT apply to:
- Straight renewals at the same federally regulated lender (as confirmed by OSFI, November 2023)
- Insured mortgages (which follow CMHC/insurer qualifying criteria)
- Mortgages from provincially regulated lenders (credit unions, most trust companies)

INCOME VERIFICATION:
FRFIs must verify borrower income through reliable documentation (T4s, NOAs, pay stubs, business financials). Self-employed income requires additional documentation.

DEBT SERVICE RATIOS:
Gross Debt Service (GDS) ratio: housing costs (mortgage P&I, property tax, heating, 50% of condo fees) / gross income. Maximum: 39%.
Total Debt Service (TDS) ratio: all debt obligations / gross income. Maximum: 44%.
These limits apply at the stressed qualifying rate.

LOAN-TO-VALUE (LTV):
Maximum LTV for uninsured mortgages: 80% (requires 20% down payment).
Properties over $1 million: cannot be insured, must be uninsured (20% minimum down).
