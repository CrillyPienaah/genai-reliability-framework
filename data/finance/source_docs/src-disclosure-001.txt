Financial Disclosure Standards and Analyst Obligations

SECURITIES DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS:
Under Canadian securities law (National Instrument 51-102, provincial Securities Acts), public companies must provide accurate, complete, and timely disclosure of material information. Misrepresentation in financial statements or analyst reports constitutes a securities violation.

A material misrepresentation is one that a reasonable investor would consider important in making an investment decision. Revenue growth of 15% vs. actual 12% is a 3 percentage point (25% relative) overstatement — this is material.

ANALYST OBLIGATIONS:
CFA Institute Code of Ethics and Standards of Professional Conduct require members to:
- Present only accurate, complete, and balanced information
- Use reasonable judgment and distinguish fact from opinion
- Not knowingly misrepresent or omit material facts

CIRO (Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization) rules require dealers and their representatives to:
- Deal fairly, honestly, and in good faith with clients
- Provide accurate information in research reports and recommendations

AUDITED FINANCIAL STATEMENTS AS AUTHORITATIVE SOURCE:
Audited financial statements, prepared under IFRS or ASPE and audited by an independent external auditor, are the authoritative source of a company's financial performance. When AI-generated summaries conflict with audited figures, the audited figures must prevail.
