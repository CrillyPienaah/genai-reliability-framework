Credit Analysis — Financial Ratios and Interpretation

LEVERAGE RATIOS:
Debt-to-Equity (D/E) = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
Measures financial leverage. Higher ratios indicate more debt relative to equity.

INDUSTRY CONTEXT IS ESSENTIAL:
- Capital-intensive industries (utilities, telecom, real estate, infrastructure): D/E ratios of 1.5-3.0x are normal and expected due to asset-heavy business models and stable cash flows supporting debt service.
- Financial institutions (banks, insurance): Highly leveraged by nature; D/E ratios of 5-15x are common.
- Technology/software companies: Generally low leverage; D/E above 0.5x may be noteworthy.
- Consumer goods/retail: Moderate leverage; D/E of 0.5-1.5x typical.

A D/E ratio of 2.5x cannot be assessed without knowing the industry. For a utility, 2.5x is moderate. For a tech company, it would be elevated.

COMPLEMENTARY METRICS:
Interest Coverage Ratio = EBIT / Interest Expense
>3x: Generally adequate debt service capacity
1-3x: Moderate; monitor closely
<1x: Cannot cover interest from operations — distress signal

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) = Operating Cash Flow / (Principal + Interest)
>1.25x: Generally acceptable for commercial lending
<1.0x: Cannot service debt from operations

Net Debt / EBITDA:
<2.0x: Conservative
2.0-4.0x: Moderate
>4.0x: Elevated; approaching speculative territory

CREDIT RATING FRAMEWORK:
Investment grade: BBB- and above (S&P/Fitch) or Baa3 and above (Moody's)
Speculative/high-yield: BB+ and below
Credit analysis should incorporate rating agency assessments alongside internal financial ratio analysis.
