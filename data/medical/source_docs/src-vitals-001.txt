Normal Vital Signs in Adults — Clinical Reference

HEART RATE:
Normal resting heart rate for adults: 60 to 100 beats per minute (bpm).
Bradycardia: heart rate below 60 bpm. May be normal in well-trained athletes (as low as 40 bpm) or pathological.
Tachycardia: heart rate above 100 bpm. Requires further evaluation if persistent.

Well-trained athletes may have a resting heart rate as low as 40 bpm due to increased vagal tone and cardiac efficiency. This is physiologically normal.

BLOOD PRESSURE:
Normal: systolic < 120 mmHg and diastolic < 80 mmHg
Elevated: systolic 120-129 mmHg and diastolic < 80 mmHg
Stage 1 hypertension: systolic 130-139 mmHg or diastolic 80-89 mmHg
Stage 2 hypertension: systolic ≥ 140 mmHg or diastolic ≥ 90 mmHg

RESPIRATORY RATE:
Normal: 12 to 20 breaths per minute
Tachypnoea: > 20 breaths per minute
Bradypnoea: < 12 breaths per minute

TEMPERATURE:
Normal oral temperature: 36.1°C to 37.2°C (97°F to 99°F)
Fever: oral temperature ≥ 38.0°C (100.4°F)

OXYGEN SATURATION (SpO2):
Normal: 95-100%
Hypoxaemia: < 95% (requires clinical evaluation)
