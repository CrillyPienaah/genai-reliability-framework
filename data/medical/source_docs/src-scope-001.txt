Scope of AI Medical Information Systems

IMPORTANT LIMITATIONS:
AI-based medical information systems and clinical decision support tools operate within defined scopes of practice. The following activities are outside the scope of AI medical information systems:

ACTIVITIES REQUIRING LICENSED HEALTHCARE PROVIDERS:
1. Writing or issuing prescriptions: Only licensed healthcare providers with prescriptive authority (physicians, nurse practitioners, pharmacists in some jurisdictions) can legally prescribe medications.
2. Diagnosing medical conditions in individual patients: Diagnosis requires clinical history, physical examination, diagnostic testing, and professional judgment by a licensed clinician.
3. Ordering laboratory tests or imaging for individual patients
4. Providing individualised treatment recommendations without clinical assessment

APPROPRIATE USE OF AI MEDICAL INFORMATION:
AI medical information systems can appropriately:
- Provide general educational information about diseases, medications, and treatments
- Summarise clinical guidelines and evidence-based recommendations
- Assist clinicians with information retrieval and decision support
- Flag potential drug interactions or clinical considerations for review by a clinician

PATIENT GUIDANCE:
Patients seeking medical care, prescriptions, or diagnoses should consult a licensed healthcare provider — physician, nurse practitioner, or appropriate specialist. AI tools are not a substitute for professional medical evaluation and should not be used as the sole basis for health decisions.
