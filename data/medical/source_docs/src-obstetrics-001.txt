Antihypertensive Medications in Pregnancy

ACE INHIBITORS AND ARBS IN PREGNANCY:
ACE inhibitors (angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors) and ARBs (angiotensin receptor blockers) are absolutely contraindicated in pregnancy, particularly in the second and third trimesters.

FDA CLASSIFICATION: Category D (second and third trimester) / Category X (some sources).

FETOPATHY (ACE inhibitor/ARB embryofetopathy):
Exposure during the second and third trimesters causes a specific pattern of fetal injury:
- Renal tubular dysplasia and renal failure
- Oligohydramnios (decreased amniotic fluid) due to fetal oliguria
- Intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR)
- Fetal and neonatal death
- Limb contractures
- Craniofacial deformities
- Pulmonary hypoplasia (due to oligohydramnios)

If a pregnant patient is found to be taking an ACE inhibitor or ARB, it should be immediately discontinued and switched to a safe alternative. Perform urgent obstetric ultrasound to assess amniotic fluid and fetal wellbeing.

SAFE ANTIHYPERTENSIVES IN PREGNANCY:
First-line options for hypertension in pregnancy:
- Labetalol (oral or IV for severe hypertension) — most widely used
- Nifedipine extended-release (oral) — well studied
- Methyldopa (oral) — long safety record, first-line in some guidelines
- Hydralazine (IV for acute severe hypertension)

Avoid: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, direct renin inhibitors, thiazides (relative contraindication).

TARGET BP IN PREGNANCY:
For chronic hypertension in pregnancy: maintain systolic 110-160 mmHg and diastolic 80-105 mmHg. Avoid aggressive lowering which may compromise uteroplacental perfusion.
